Famous place in Caen

Caen is a city in northwestern France, the seat of the regional council of the Normandy region and the capital of the Calvados department. It has a population of 403,633.

    World Heritage site Mont Saint-Michel

    The history of Mont Saint-Michel goes back a long way. In the 11th century, the Romanesque monastery was built, and in the 13th century, the Gothic Ramelbeil was built. In 1979, Mont Saint-Michel and its bay were registered as a World Heritage Site.

    Mémorial de Caen

    The Mémorial de Caen is a museum in Caen dedicated to 20th-century history and peace. The Mémorial de Caen has been awarded the label "Musée de France "2 and is a member of the International Network of Museums for Peace3.

    Le château de Caen

    Built by William the Conqueror around 1060, the ducal castle became the favorite residence of the Dukes of Normandy, the kings of England, and was one of the largest fortresses in Europe.

Manner and Habits of France

  • Dining Etiquette

    It is good manners to eat with a fork in your left hand and a knife in your right.

  • unpunctual

    It is normal to be later than the scheduled time. When eating lunch, the French can be about three hours late.

  • Shopping

    In France, there are no 24-hour stores like convenience stores. The market is closed on Sundays.
